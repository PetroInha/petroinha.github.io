## Oil Sands Technology: Advanced Reservoir Simulation

The first session featured Prof. Hyundon Shin's presentation on **"Oil Sands Production Technology and Simulation."** Oil sands — a complex mixture of oil, sand, and clay — require high-temperature, high-pressure steam injection (SAGD method) to reduce viscosity and improve productivity.

<div class="post-quote">"Accurate prediction of how steam spreads and how the formation responds determines production performance — and simulation precision will decide everything."</div>

Key points highlighted:
- High initial CAPEX → prediction failure carries enormous risk
- Lab vs. field data discrepancy → history matching techniques are essential
- Importance of accurate petrophysical property-based modeling

## CCS, Lithium, Hydrogen — The Spectrum of Energy Engineering

Subsequent sessions covered a wide range of next-generation energy technologies:

- SLB's **thermal simulation advancement**
- **CCS database construction and Cross-border M&A trends** by KIGAM and KNOC
- **Direct Lithium Extraction (DLE)** from brine formations
- **Hydrogen and unconventional resource development technologies**
- Case studies on industry-academia collaborative research and international cooperation

## Message from Prof. Hyundon Shin

<div class="post-quote">"Even in the era of carbon neutrality, unconventional resource development technologies remain critically important for energy security and key material procurement."</div>

This workshop was a powerful reminder that the scope of energy engineering is broad, the challenges are real, and the opportunities for students and researchers alike are greater than ever.
